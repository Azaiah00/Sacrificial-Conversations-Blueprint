PODCAST BLUEPRINT: SACRIFICIAL CONVERSATIONS

Hosts: Teddy & Monica
Guest: Dr. Lametra Scott, PharmD (Founder, Breaking the SSickle Cell Cycle Foundation)
Target Run Time: 60 - 75 Minutes
Core Theme: Faith Over Fear, Medical Advocacy, Motherhood, & Purpose in the Pain

🎙️ I. EPISODE LOGLINE (The Pitch)

When the medical system failed her newborn son, Dr. Lametra Scott didn't just get angry—she built a foundation. This episode explores the devastating shock of a misdiagnosis, the daily sacrifices of raising a child with Sickle Cell Disease, and how a mother's faith transformed her greatest trial into a lifesaving movement.

🎬 II. THE COLD OPEN (0:00 - 1:00)

Producer Note: Record this intro after the interview, or pull the heaviest hitting quote from the interview to play before the theme music.

Suggested Cold Open Clip:
Dr. Scott: (Answering how it felt to get the real diagnosis from the state)
Teddy (V/O): "Dr. Scott, you are a medical professional. Yet, when your son was born, the medical system gave you the wrong diagnosis about a life-threatening condition. What exactly went through your mind when the State Health Department called to tell you your pediatrician was wrong?"
[CUT TO SHOW INTRO/THEME MUSIC]

⏱️ III. RUN OF SHOW (ROS) & SEGMENT BREAKDOWN

SEGMENT 1: The Setup & The Spirit (1:00 - 15:00)

Objective: Introduce Dr. Scott, establish the spiritual foundation, and discuss the initial fear of the unknown.

Host Cue (Monica): Welcome the audience, introduce Dr. Scott's impressive credentials (PharmD, CCHP), and pivot immediately to her role as a mother.

Key Story Beats: Finding out she and Rickey's father were carriers; defying the odds; her spiritual conviction during pregnancy.

The "Viral" Questions:

(Teddy): "When you get a statistic like a '1 in 4 chance' of a severe, chronic disease, logic screams one thing, but faith says another. How did you silence the noise of the medical statistics to trust that God makes no mistakes?"

(Monica): "Take us back to Jeremiah 1:5. How did the promise of 'Before I formed thee in the womb I knew thee' anchor you when the world was telling you to panic?"

SEGMENT 2: The Shock & The Systemic Failure (15:00 - 30:00)

Objective: The emotional climax of the story. Exposing the flaw in the healthcare system and the visceral reaction of a mother.

Key Story Beats: The pediatrician falsely stating Rickey was "only a carrier." The jarring phone call from the TN Health Department confirming SS Sickle Cell Disease. The realization that doctors lack SCD education.

The "Viral" Questions:

(Teddy): "You're a pharmacist. You understand medicine better than most. How did it shatter your perspective of the healthcare system when it failed your own son right out of the gate?"

(Monica): "Put us in the room with you. You've said getting that phone call felt like getting the wind knocked out of you. How did you pull yourself off the floor and transition from a grieving mother into a warrior advocate?"

SEGMENT 3: The Mechanics & The Sacrifices (30:00 - 45:00)

Objective: Educate the audience on what Sickle Cell actually is, and highlight the daily, unseen sacrifices of parents raising children with chronic illnesses.

Key Story Beats: Breaking down the disease (sticky, banana-shaped cells causing tissue damage). Rickey's challenges (Dactylitis, blood transfusions at age 3). The daily pivots (wetsuits in pools, martial arts instead of high-impact sports).

Viral Moment — Rickey's Banana Quote: Rickey explained his condition to his kindergarten teacher: "My blood cells are sticky and shaped like bananas, not like donuts. They stick together and make my legs hurt." (Source: 360 SCD Hub) — Use this exact moment; it humanizes the disease for viewers.

Additional Story Beats (from research): Acute chest syndrome and blood transfusion at age 3 (leading cause of death for SCD patients). Enuresis (bedwetting) and potty training as an unseen sacrifice—SCD affects kidneys' ability to concentrate urine. Martial arts instead of soccer (short bursts vs constant running); baseball; heated pools and wetsuit for swimming. Dr. Michael DeBaun (hematologist) and nurse Jeannie Byrd provided the patient handbook that helped Lametra early on.

The "Viral" Questions:

(Teddy): "For those who don't understand the invisible pain of this disease, can you break down the actual mechanics? What is physically happening in a child's body during a pain crisis?"

(Teddy): "Rickey told his kindergarten teacher his blood cells are sticky and shaped like bananas. When did you realize he understood his condition enough to explain it to others?"

(Monica): "You refuse to let the disease rob Rickey of his childhood. How do you balance being fiercely protective as a mother without letting fear completely take over his life?"

(Monica): "You guest-lecture at pharmacy schools now—UT, Lipscomb. What do you tell future pharmacists that you wish your son's pediatrician had known?"

SEGMENT 4: Breaking the Cycle & Purpose (45:00 - 55:00)

Objective: Transition from pain to purpose. Discuss her foundation and her "Why."

Key Story Beats: Founding the Breaking The SSickle Cell Cycle Foundation (BTSSCC). Bridging the education gap for patients and medical professionals. Changing the narrative.

The "Viral" Questions:

(Teddy): "You literally built an organization to fix the exact gap in the system that hurt your family. What is the ultimate vision for 'Breaking the Cycle'?"

(Monica): "How long did it take for you to zoom out and see that Rickey's diagnosis wasn't a punishment, but a divine platform?"

SEGMENT 5: The "Sacrificial" Close (55:00 - 65:00)

Objective: Land the plane. Tie the entire interview back to the core theme of the podcast. High emotional resonance.

The "Viral" Questions:

(Teddy): "Dr. Scott, looking back at 2012 to now, what is the greatest sacrifice you've had to make on this journey, and what did that sacrifice buy you?"

(Monica): "If you could look directly into the camera right now and speak to a mother who just got the phone call that her baby has a chronic illness... what is the first thing she needs to hear?"

🎧 IV. PRODUCER CUES FOR TEDDY & MONICA

The "Pivot to Expertise": (Teddy) When Dr. Scott shares a deeply emotional story, pivot to her authority. "As a mother, that's heartbreaking to watch, but as a Pharmacist, how did that drive you to go and educate other doctors?"

The "Faith Check": (Monica) When the medical terminology gets a bit too clinical, anchor it back to the spirit. "Through all those hospital visits and blood transfusions, where did you feel God in the room with you?"

Support System Callout: When Dr. Scott mentions her hematologist or early education, name-drop Dr. Michael DeBaun and nurse Jeannie Byrd—they provided the patient handbook that helped her. This validates her support system and adds credibility.

Concrete Examples: When discussing daily life with Rickey, draw out heated pools and wetsuit for swimming as concrete examples of adaptations. These visuals resonate with parents of kids with chronic conditions.

The Pregnant Pause: When Dr. Scott talks about the moment she found out about the diagnosis, let her sit in the silence. Do not rush to fill the dead air. Let the emotion breathe.
